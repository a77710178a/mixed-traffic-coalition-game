## Paper 1: Liu et al. 2026, Transportation Research Part C

**Title:** A cooperative decision-making method for CAVs from the perspective of opinion dynamics

**Source:** Transportation Research Part C: Emerging Technologies, Vol. 182, Article 105412, published January 2026.

**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1016/j.trc.2025.105412

**Accessible evidence used:** local PDF page rendering and visual reading from `docs/literature/page_images/liu_2026_opinion_dynamics/`.

### Extraction Matrix

| Field | Confirmed / current note |
| --- | --- |
| Scenario | Confirmed: four-arm, dual-lane unsignalized roundabout with 24 conflict points. The paper argues roundabouts have more weaving conflicts than traditional intersections. |
| Vehicle mix | Confirmed: CAVs and HVs. Simulation varies CAV penetration rates: 0%, 25%, 50%, 75%, 100%. HIL includes CAV-only and mixed-traffic cases, with at most one HV due to hardware limitations. |
| Decision object | Confirmed: rights-of-way / passing sequence and time window through conflict zones. Final vehicle motion is implemented by rolling optimization/MPC-like control. |
| Method core | Confirmed: opinion dynamics-based negotiation framework. It has four steps: opinion formation, opinion quantification, opinion consensus, and opinion implementation. Feasible passing sequences are generated by breadth-first search over conflict zones. |
| HDV uncertainty | Confirmed: HV behavior is handled through parameter calibration and intention classification. The paper calibrates headway time parameters from INTERACTION data and uses SVM to classify whether an HV will preempt or yield. |
| Deep learning / learning component | Confirmed: machine learning exists, but it is SVM, not deep learning. Inputs are four hand-crafted metrics based on speed, distance to conflict point, TTCP, and remaining-space relations. Reported SVM accuracy is 93.89%, with F1 0.96 for "priority for inside" and 0.86 for "priority for outside." |
| Safety mechanism | Confirmed: time windows are designed with TTC risk threshold logic; safety evaluation uses hazardous delta-TTCP proportion and emergency/hazardous deceleration frequency. Emergency braking is used as a foundational layer. No PET-based safety metric was observed in the read pages. |
| Fairness/social rule | Social customs are considered through HV yielding/preempting intentions. No explicit waiting fairness, Gini coefficient, tail-delay fairness, or payoff fairness was observed. |
| Cooperation concept | Confirmed: cooperative decision-making and consensus among CAVs. This is not a cooperative game or coalition game in the main formulation. It uses marginal contribution only to define influence weights in the consensus update, not Shapley/payoff allocation. |
| Baselines | Confirmed: rule-based method and game-based method. The paper compares Consensus / CA against Rule and Game under CAV penetration-rate settings. |
| Evaluation strength | Strong for its claim. It uses TESSNG traffic simulation, CAV penetration-rate experiments, convergence/runtime analysis, safety, delay, comfort, fuel, and CARLA/ROS2 HIL validation. |
| What is already covered | Mixed-traffic roundabout cooperation, CAV right-of-way negotiation, BFS-based passing-sequence generation, SVM-based HV yielding/preempting classification, opinion consensus, rolling motion control, TESSNG simulation, and HIL validation. |
| Remaining gap for our paper | The paper does not appear to cover cooperative-game coalition formation, Shapley/nucleolus/Nash bargaining, explicit fairness allocation, or graph deep learning prediction. It is roundabout-focused rather than general unsignalized intersection-focused. Our viable gap remains conflict-zone graph coalition valuation, interaction-aware deep prediction, and fairness-aware passing-right allocation. |

### Collision Risk With Our Idea

**High.**

This paper covers several ingredients that overlap with our revised direction:

- mixed traffic,
- unsignalized setting,
- right-of-way negotiation / passing sequence selection,
- SVM-based HV intention prediction,
- social compliance,
- rolling optimization,
- strong experimental validation.

It does **not** appear to claim:

- cooperative-game coalition formation,
- cooperative-game allocation such as Shapley/nucleolus/Nash bargaining,
- learned risk probability entering coalition value,
- fairness metrics such as waiting-time inequality.

These are our current possible differentiation points.

### Implication For Our Paper

Avoid saying:

- "first learning-enhanced cooperative decision-making method,"
- "first to integrate HDV intention prediction into mixed-traffic unsignalized control,"
- "first right-of-way negotiation method for CAVs and HDVs."

Safer positioning:

> Unlike opinion-dynamics consensus methods that treat passing sequences as opinions among communicating CAVs, our method formulates conflict-zone-level vehicle groups as dynamic coalitions and uses interaction-aware HDV intention/risk estimates to construct a risk-constrained coalition value and fairness-aware passing-right allocation.

### Residual Checks

- If we cite it in the manuscript, verify exact table values and baseline names from the final PDF.
- If we use INTERACTION as our dataset, avoid copying the same hand-crafted SVM setup as the main learning novelty.
- If we use roundabouts as an experiment, this paper becomes a much stronger baseline threat. A four-leg unsignalized intersection may give cleaner differentiation.

## Paper 2: Cui et al. 2026, IEEE Internet of Things Journal

**Title:** A Game-Theoretic Framework of Interaction and Cooperative Driving for CAVs at Mixed Unsignalized Intersections

**Source:** IEEE Internet of Things Journal, Vol. 13, No. 1, January 2026.

**Accessible evidence used:** local PDF full-text extraction from `docs/literature/pdfs/`.

### Extraction Matrix

| Field | Confirmed / current note |
| --- | --- |
| Scenario | Confirmed: mixed unsignalized intersections, with simulation cases involving a cross intersection and later CARLA-based HIL/Human-in-the-loop scenarios. The simulation includes four entrance lanes and scenarios with 2, 4, 8, and 12 vehicles. |
| Vehicle mix | Confirmed: CAVs and HDVs coexist. Vehicle types are randomly assigned in scalability experiments. |
| Decision object | Confirmed: cooperative driving actions for CAVs, including acceleration/deceleration actions and conflict resolution through cooperation/interactions. |
| Method core | Confirmed: cooperative game theory for CAV-CAV cooperation plus Stackelberg game for CAV-HDV interaction. The models are integrated through bi-level programming. |
| HDV uncertainty | Confirmed: the paper explicitly targets HDV behavioral uncertainty. It uses a twin-game-based parameter updating mechanism to adjust HDV-related reward-function weights dynamically. |
| Deep learning / learning component | Confirmed: the method is not primarily deep learning. It compares against RL methods such as I-PPO/singlePPO, but the proposed method itself is game-theoretic. |
| Safety mechanism | Confirmed: reward function considers safety, efficiency, and comfort. Safety constraints are included. Evaluation uses PET and TDTC, with PET values analyzed statistically. |
| Fairness/social rule | Partially covered but not as fairness metrics. The paper discusses individual rationality and group rationality in the cooperative game. No clear evidence found yet for Shapley value, nucleolus, Nash bargaining, Gini, or waiting-time fairness metrics. |
| Cooperation concept | Confirmed: cooperative game, coalition concept, superadditivity, individual rationality, group rationality, and cooperative subgroup division. Subgroups are formed by selecting the vehicle closest to the intersection center as anchor and matching closest vehicles from entry lanes, forming groups of 1-4 vehicles. |
| Baselines | Confirmed: FIFO, PIDM, singlePPO, I-PPO, G-Nontwins, and G-Intwins variants. |
| Evaluation strength | Strong. Numerical simulations plus CARLA-based hardware-in-the-loop and human-in-the-loop validation. Metrics include average speed, success rate, PET, and TDTC. |
| What is already covered | Mixed unsignalized intersections, CAV-HDV interaction, cooperative game, Stackelberg game, HDV uncertainty, coalition/subgroup division, PET-based safety evaluation, RL comparison, HIL/Human-in-loop validation. |
| Remaining gap for our paper | The safest remaining space is not "game-theoretic mixed unsignalized intersection control." It is narrower: interaction-aware deep prediction of HDV intention/risk feeding a risk-constrained coalition value, plus explicit fairness-aware allocation of passing rights or coalition benefits. Vehicle-infrastructure-assisted prediction may also help separate our setting. |

### Collision Risk With Our Idea

**Very high.**

This paper directly overlaps with:

- mixed traffic at unsignalized intersections,
- cooperative game theory,
- CAV-CAV cooperation,
- CAV-HDV game interaction,
- HDV uncertainty modeling,
- coalition/subgroup concepts,
- safety-efficiency reward design,
- PET-based evaluation,
- RL baselines.

It weakens any novelty claim framed as:

- "a game-theoretic framework for mixed unsignalized intersections,"
- "cooperative game for CAVs at mixed unsignalized intersections,"
- "CAV-HDV interaction using game theory,"
- "coalition/subgroup-based cooperative driving."

### Implication For Our Paper

The paper's presence forces our idea to become more specific and more learning-centered, but still not black-box:

> Our differentiator should be an interaction-aware deep prediction module, preferably GRU + graph attention or temporal graph learning, that estimates HDV intention/risk under vehicle-infrastructure observations. These estimates should enter a risk-constrained coalition value and an explicit fairness-aware payoff/passing-right allocation mechanism, rather than only optimizing total group reward.

Avoid relying on subgroup formation alone. Cui et al. already has cooperative subgroup division.

Potential remaining novelty angles:

1. **Prediction mechanism:** graph deep learning for HDV intention, arrival uncertainty, and risk probability, rather than twin-game parameter updating.
2. **Coalition valuation:** explicit coalition value over conflict-zone graph using predicted risk and uncertainty penalties.
3. **Fair allocation:** Shapley/nucleolus/Nash bargaining or a computational approximation for passing-right/fairness allocation.
4. **Evaluation gap:** waiting-time fairness, tail delay, Gini coefficient, and robustness to prediction noise/observation noise.
5. **V2I setting:** roadside perception and communication reliability as part of the prediction/control loop.

### Required Follow-Up Checks

- Inspect equations around cooperative game reward and subgroup division to see whether they already define a coalition value comparable to ours.
- Inspect whether the subgroup division is conflict-zone-based or only proximity/entry-lane based.
- Inspect whether any payoff allocation rule exists beyond maximizing total group reward.
- Inspect whether waiting fairness or tail-delay fairness is absent.
